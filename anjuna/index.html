Anjuna Beach in North Goa is famous for its lively atmosphere and scenic beauty. Known as a hub for the hippie culture since the 1960s, it's a place where you can relax by day and enjoy vibrant beach parties by night.
The beach is lined with shacks offering tasty local food and drinks. Every Wednesday, the Anjuna Flea Market attracts visitors with its colorful stalls selling clothes, jewelry, and souvenirs. The beach is also great for water sports like parasailing and jet skiing. With its mix of natural charm and energetic vibe, Anjuna Beach offers a unique experience, perfect for those looking to unwind or explore Goa’s vibrant culture.

How to Reach :
From Goa International Airport (Dabolim Airport):
By Taxi: The easiest way is to take a taxi. It's about 45 kilometers away and takes around 1 to 1.5 hours.
By Bus: You can take a bus to Panaji, then transfer to another bus heading to Anjuna.

From Manohar International Airport (Mopa Airport):
By Taxi: The beach is about 28 kilometers away, and a taxi ride will take around 45 minutes to 1 hour.
By Bus: Take a bus to Mapusa, then switch to a local bus to Anjuna Beach.

From Thivim Railway Station:
By Taxi or Auto-Rickshaw: Anjuna Beach is 19 kilometers away, with a travel time of about 30 to 40 minutes.

From Madgaon (Margao) Railway Station:
By Taxi: The beach is 52 kilometers away, and the drive takes about 1.5 to 2 hours.
By Train and Bus: You can take a train to Thivim and then a bus or taxi to Anjuna.

Best Time to Visit :

  • November to March: Best time to visit. The weather is warm and dry, with clear skies. Enjoy festivals and many events, perfect for beach activities and exploration.
  • April to May: Very hot, often above 35°C (95°F). Quieter with fewer tourists, suitable for budget travelers if you can handle the heat.
  • June to September: Monsoon season with heavy rain and rough seas. The beach may be less accessible, and some places might be closed. Not ideal for beach activities, but the lush scenery and lower prices may appeal to some.
  • October: Weather is clearing up from the monsoon. The beach is less crowded, offering a quieter experience if you want to avoid peak season crowds.

    • St Antony's Church
    St. Anthony's Church in Anjuna Beach is a simple and beautiful church known for its calm and peaceful feel. The church has white walls and a traditional look, making it a nice spot to visit if you're in the area. It’s dedicated to St. Anthony, a well-loved saint. The church is not very big, but it’s a quiet place to take a break from the busy beach. If you enjoy seeing local landmarks and want a moment of calm during your trip, this church is worth a visit.

    Anjuna Flea Market
    Anjuna Flea Market is a popular market held every Wednesday near Anjuna Beach in Goa. It is well-known for its vibrant and lively atmosphere. The market features a wide variety of stalls selling clothes, jewelry, accessories, and souvenirs.
    You can also find unique handmade items and local crafts. The market is a great place to shop for gifts and enjoy delicious street food. Many visitors come here to experience the local culture and enjoy the bustling vibe. Whether you're looking to buy something special or just want to explore and soak up the lively scene, Anjuna Flea Market is a must-visit spot.

    Night Life
    Nightlife at Anjuna Beach is lively and exciting, offering a range of activities for evening fun. The beach is famous for its vibrant parties and music events, especially at popular spots like Curlies and Shiva Valley. These venues feature energetic music, dancing, and a relaxed, bohemian atmosphere. Anjuna also has many beachfront bars and cafes where you can enjoy cocktails and meet people from around the world. The nightlife scene often includes themed parties and live performances, adding to the lively vibe. Whether you’re into dancing all night or just enjoying a drink by the sea, Anjuna Beach provides a memorable and enjoyable nightlife experience

    Water Sports
    Parasailing: Fly high above the sea, enjoying breathtaking aerial views as you are towed by a boat.
    Jet Skiing: Experience the thrill of riding a jet ski over the waves, perfect for adventure seekers.
    Banana Boat Rides: Hold on tight as you and a group are pulled behind a boat on a large inflatable banana for a fun and bumpy ride.
    Kayaking: Paddle through the calm waters and explore the coastline at your own pace, enjoying a relaxing and scenic experience.

    Snow Park
    The Snow Park Goa, located near Anjuna Beach in the Tito's Lane area, offers a unique escape from the beachside heat. You can enjoy activities like snow slides, snowball fights, and even experience artificial snowfall. It's just a short distance away, making it a convenient and fun option for cooling off after a day at the beach.

     Seafood: Enjoy fresh and delicious seafood dishes like grilled fish, prawns, and calamari.
    Goan Cuisine: Try local favorites such as Goan fish curry, bebinca (a traditional dessert), and vindaloo.
    International Dishes: Many places offer a variety of international dishes like pasta, burgers, and pizza.
    Street Food: Sample tasty street food options such as samosas, vada pav, and chaat.

    Where to Eat:

    Shiva Valley: Famous for its lively atmosphere and tasty dishes, including Goan and international cuisine.
    Anjuna Flea Market Stalls: Find a variety of street food options while shopping at the market.
    The Mango Tree: A charming restaurant offering Goan and continental dishes in a cozy setting.
